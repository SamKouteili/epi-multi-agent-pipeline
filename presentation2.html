Expanding the EPI's Data Horizons

An AI-powered pipeline to discover new data sources for hard-to-measure environmental indicators

YCELP × ROSE Lab  •  2026

The Core Idea

What Is a Data Proxy?

A data proxy is a variable that tracks something we can't easily measure directly. When two phenomena are causally linked, measuring one tells us about the other.

We are primarily interested in mechanistic causal explanations, where we have a reason why two variables move together.

For the EPI

Many of the 58 EPI indicators suffer from poor country coverage, infrequent updates, or reliance on modeled data. Validated proxies can fill these gaps with real, observed data.

COVID-19 & Wastewater

During the pandemic, countries discovered that SARS-CoV-2 concentrations in sewage predicted infection surges days before clinical testing data. Wastewater became a proxy for case counts — cheaper, faster, and more comprehensive than individual testing.

Pharmaceutical Sales & Water Quality

Similarly, anti-diarrheal medication sales rise when drinking water quality deteriorates. Sales data is reported monthly at the national level — far more frequently than direct water testing programs.

The Challenge

How the EPI Could Benefit from Data Proxies

The EPI ranks 180 countries across 58+ indicators. But many indicators rely on imputed or modeled data, especially in waste management and water quality:

The goal: find alternative, more accessible data sources that correlate with these hard-to-measure indicators and could supplement or validate the official statistics.

The Opportunity

Modern AI can systematically search scientific literature, identify causal mechanisms, and test hypothesized proxy relationships against real data — scaling up a process that would take researchers months to do manually.

The Bigger Picture

Building a Knowledge Graph of Proxy Relationships

We have no ground truth to verify our proxy discoveries against, but we can use the relationships we find to validate claims against one another. Each verified hypothesis becomes an edge in a growing knowledge graph. Nodes are data variables; edges carry direction, strength, and functional form.

  • Sign consistency — If A→B is positive and B→C is positive, then A→C should be positive too
  • Confounder detection — If an edge disappears after controlling for a third variable, it's spurious
  • Strength bounds — Strong chains can't produce near-zero direct links

Enrichment

Grounding with Domain Context

Before the research agent begins, we provide it with expert knowledge from the EPI Technical Appendix, so it doesn't rediscover what the team knows.

What each context entry includes:

  • Indicator definition and how it's computed
  • Current imputation model and its limitations
  • Known confounders to control for
  • Data quality issues and caveats
  • Related "sibling" indicators in the EPI
UWD Context Provided

"UWD measures age-standardized DALYs lost per 100k due to unsafe drinking water. Source: IHME Global Burden of Disease, 1990–2021. Key confounders: GDP per capita, urbanization, healthcare access. Sibling indicators: USD, HFD (same IHME method)."

Why this matters

Without this context, the AI might propose GDP as a "novel proxy" — wasting a verification cycle on a known confounder. Domain context steers the research toward genuinely useful discoveries.

Stage 1

Deep Research Agent

Building on the injected domain context, an AI research agent conducts a thorough literature review and data discovery for the target indicator. It maps causal pathways, finds published proxy relationships, and brainstorms novel candidates.

What the agent investigates:

  • Causal pathways — What drives this indicator up or down?
  • Published proxies — Has anyone studied alternative measurements?
  • Novel candidates — What related data exists in public databases?
  • Data availability — How many countries? How recent?
  • Confounders — What might create spurious correlations?
UWD Research Output

The agent produced a 2,000-word report identifying 14 candidate proxies for Unsafe Drinking Water, including:

  • Healthcare facility handwashing rates (WHO)
  • Basic sanitation service coverage (JMP)
  • Under-5 mortality from diarrheal disease (IHME)
  • Water stress levels (FAO AQUASTAT)
  • Environmental governance scores (WGI)

Validation Agent

After each exploration agent finishes, a validation agent reviews its work — an automated peer review step.

Why two agents?

In early runs, the exploration agent sometimes misinterpreted WHO data breakdowns or merged on the wrong key. The validation agent catches these systematic errors before they reach the final report.

Stage 2 — Toolkit

Agent Toolkit & Guardrails

The agent has access to a curated set of tools. It composes a verification script from these building blocks and executes it:

Database Access
World Bank (20k+ indicators), WHO Global Health Observatory, 312 local EPI datasets
Statistical Models
Bivariate correlation, partial correlation (controlling for GDP, HDI), functional form testing
Data Operations
Country name alignment, temporal matching, sentinel value handling, merging datasets
Verdict Logic
Standardized decision rules for confirmed, partially confirmed, rejected, or inconclusive

Key Guardrails

No data fabrication

The agent is never allowed to generate synthetic data. If it cannot access a dataset, the hypothesis is marked inconclusive.

Mandatory confounder controls

Every correlation is tested with partial correlation, controlling for GDP, HDI, urbanization, and population.

Consistent output format

All agents use a shared statistical library, ensuring identical methods and output formats across all hypotheses.

Looking Ahead

Next Steps

Scale Up
Run the pipeline across all 58 EPI indicators, starting with Tier 1 (waste & wastewater) where data gaps are most severe
Unconventional Sources
Expand to satellite imagery, vessel tracking, consumer purchasing data, crowdsourced sensors, and search trends
Knowledge Graph
Build the full graph structure with automated consistency checking — sign transitivity, confounder detection, strength bounds
Richer Statistics
Add log-Y transforms, polynomial fits, threshold models, and Granger causality for temporal proxy relationships
Regional Context
Test region-specific proxy relationships (e.g. "in Sub-Saharan Africa, X correlates with Y") beyond global patterns
EPI Integration
Feed validated proxies back into the EPI pipeline to improve imputation models and expand country coverage
